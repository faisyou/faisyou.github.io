Genesys AVA Workshop — Assignment Spec

Voxi · Movie Night Planner

A Genesys Agentic Virtual Agent (AVA) for Vox Cinema Dubai. It takes a guest from "what's playing?" through movie, showtime, and food selection, tracking spend against a budget the whole way, and finishes with a checkout that hands back a payment code.

Build target: AVA Definition Tool type: Data Action Tools: 8 Stages: 3 Terminal actions: 1 Custom guardrails: 4

01 · Mission

What this agent is responsible for

Internalize these before writing a single tool instruction — every instruction you write should serve one of them.

Discovery

Help the guest find a movie and showtime that fits their taste and schedule.

Budget

The moment a budget is mentioned, open a cart and track spend after every addition.

Pace

Ask one question at a time, and never re-ask something already answered.

Transparency

Always mention prices, and warn before anything would push the guest over budget.

Consent

Confirm before adding, removing, or checking out — never before just searching or looking something up.

Scope

Stay inside movie-night planning. Politely decline anything outside it.

Out of scope — existing booking changes/cancellations · group bookings & special events · refunds & payment disputes · facility issues or anything not booking-related.

03 · Tool Inspector

Interface detail

Source key — User: filled in from the conversation. ToolInput: passed along from earlier in the session. ToolOutput: automatically mapped from a specific field of an earlier tool's output (see Notes for which one).

Your assignment: build a complete, working Genesys Agentic Virtual Agent (AVA) Definition for this movie-night planning flow — the role definition, the instructions (how to normalize loose language like "Friday" or "scary movies", how to pace the conversation), the pre-instructions for each tool (when and how to call it, what to confirm with the guest first), the outcome instructions for each tool (how the agent reacts to every possible result — found, not found, over budget, error), and any guardrails the agent needs. The tool schema and their Data Actions shown above are predefined — use them exactly as they are; do not modify the tooling structure. Your job is the AVA definition that wires these eight tools together into a complete, functioning agent.

Scope note — checkout_cart returns a payment_code once the guest confirms, and that's the finish line for this exercise. Whatever happens after that point (redirecting to the payment platform, confirming the transaction, bringing the guest back to the conversation) is intentionally left out of scope — it's included here as a challenge exercise for discussion, not something you need to build. Implement just far enough to get a payment code back from the AVA.
