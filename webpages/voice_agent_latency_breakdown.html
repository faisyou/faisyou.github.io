Human turn-taking in natural conversation averages 200 – 400 ms gap between speakers. Cognitive science research places the "uncomfortable silence" threshold at around 500 ms, and anything beyond 800 ms begins to feel like a broken call.

Engineering levers to reduce end-to-end latency

Streaming STT with early commit
Use streaming ASR (e.g. NVIDIA Nemotron Speech, Deepgram) that emits partial transcripts. Fire LLM request on high-confidence partial, cancel if user continues. Saves 200–400 ms.
STT
Aggressive VAD tuning
Reduce end-of-speech silence threshold from default 500 ms to 150–200 ms. Combine with semantic turn-detection (model predicts if utterance is complete) to avoid false cuts.
VAD
LLM speculative decoding
Use a small draft model to predict tokens, verified by main model in parallel. Achieves 2–4× throughput improvement. Reduces TTFT on large models like 70B+ from 600 ms to ~200 ms.
LLM
Co-location & edge inference
Deploy STT, LLM, and TTS in the same datacenter region. Use WebRTC or QUIC instead of HTTP/REST. Eliminates inter-service hops. Cuts network contribution from 80 ms to <20 ms.
Network
Streaming TTS with first-chunk play
Start audio playback on the very first TTS chunk (typically 1–2 sentences). Use low-latency TTS like NVIDIA MagpieTTS or ElevenLabs Turbo. Reduce perceived delay by 150–300 ms.
TTS
KV-cache & prompt caching
Pre-fill the LLM's KV-cache with the system prompt and conversation history. Subsequent turns only process the new user utterance. Can cut TTFT from 500 ms to 150 ms for long contexts.
LLM
Barge-in / interrupt handling
Detect mid-response speech with a fast VAD bypass. Immediately cancel TTS playback and LLM generation. Frees GPU sooner and makes the agent feel naturally interruptible rather than robotic.
Architecture
Smaller, quantised models
For simple intents (FAQs, slot filling), route to a fine-tuned 7B–30B quantised model (INT4/INT8) instead of a frontier model. Achieves TTFT of <100 ms. Reserve large models for complex turns.

Challenges of end-to-end speech LLMs

No tool-calling support
E2E models can't easily invoke external functions (CRM lookup, booking APIs). Cascaded pipelines intercept text tokens mid-generation to fire tools — audio models don't expose this hook cleanly.
Guardrails & compliance
In CCaaS, every utterance may need PCI/GDPR screening, profanity filtering, or topic guardrails. Text-based pipelines intercept at the transcript layer; audio-out models lack equivalent interception points.
Factual accuracy & hallucination
Cascaded systems use RAG on the text transcript to ground responses. E2E audio models have no native RAG path — grounding audio generation in retrieved knowledge remains unsolved at scale.
Multilingual & domain data scarcity
Training requires large volumes of unscripted, multi-speaker conversational audio. Fisher Corpus used by PersonaPlex is English-only. Enterprise domains (banking, aviation, telecom) have almost no equivalent data.
Limited fine-tuning control
Cascaded pipelines allow independent fine-tuning of STT, LLM, and TTS per domain. Audio models entangle all three — changing business logic requires retraining the whole model.
Eval & observability
Transcript-based systems enable word-level QA, intent classification, sentiment scoring. Audio-out models produce waveforms — evaluating response quality and auditing calls requires an extra ASR pass.

For enterprise CCaaS today, the streaming cascade remains the pragmatic choice — PersonaPlex and similar models are production-ready for conversational AI companions and demos, but the missing tool-call / RAG / guardrail layer means they're not drop-in replacements for Genesys-style deployments yet.
