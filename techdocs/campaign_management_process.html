WhatsApp Campaign Management

Process Walkthrough

Overview Slide 1

High-level view of how a B2B Customer uses the Campaign Provider Portal to run WhatsApp campaigns, while the Campaign Provider and Genesys Cloud handle execution behind the scenes.

Parties Involved
Roles
  • B2B Customer – business that initiates and configures WhatsApp campaigns.
  • Campaign Provider – owns customer data, executes campaigns, and masks phone numbers.
  • Genesys Cloud – outbound engine that manages calling lists and sends WhatsApp messages.
Key Principles
  • Only pre-approved WhatsApp templates can be sent.
  • B2B Customer never sees the underlying phone numbers or calling list.
  • Campaign Provider maintains a per-B2B Do Not Contact (DNC) list for WhatsApp.

Behind the Scenes: Genesys Cloud Slide 3

What happens once the B2B Customer hits "Launch" — how the Campaign Provider and Genesys Cloud collaborate to create and start the outbound WhatsApp campaign.

Campaign Creation & Calling List
  • Portal sends launch request to Campaign Provider backend.
  • Backend:
    • Resolves filters into a concrete customer list.
    • Excludes any customers on the B2B DNC list.
    • Creates a Genesys Outbound Campaign via API.
    • Creates / populates a Genesys Calling List.
  • Each calling list record includes:
    • Contact identifier (internal ID, not shown to B2B Customer).
    • WhatsApp-enabled phone field.
    • Template ID + personalization parameters (if any).
Campaign Execution View
  • Campaign Provider starts the campaign in Genesys Cloud.
  • B2B Customer sees only:
    • Total contacts selected / targeted.
    • Status (Running / Completed / Failed).
    • Optional: basic delivery and interaction metrics.
  • Actual sending is handled by Genesys → WhatsApp integration.

Customer WhatsApp Experience Slide 4

The end-customer receives a WhatsApp template message containing three action buttons: Interested, Not Interested, and Opt Out.

Interactive WhatsApp Message
Buttons in the Template
  • Interested – customer wants to engage.
  • Not Interested – customer declines the offer.
  • Opt Out – customer does not want to be contacted again on WhatsApp for this B2B Customer.

Technical Handling
  • WhatsApp button click → Genesys receives callback.
  • Genesys calls Campaign Provider APIs per interaction.
Example Callback Design
// Interested
POST /campaigns/<campaignId>/customer/<customerId>/interested

// Not Interested
POST /campaigns/<campaignId>/customer/<customerId>/not-interested

// Opt Out (DNC)
POST /campaigns/<campaignId>/customer/<customerId>/optout

// Body (example)
{
  "interactionId": "<genesysInteractionId>",
  "timestamp": "2025-01-01T12:34:56Z",
  "channel": "whatsapp"
}

DNC & Responsibilities Slide 5

How Opt-Out is enforced and who is responsible for what across B2B Customer, Campaign Provider, and Genesys Cloud.

Do Not Contact (DNC) Logic
  • Opt Out button adds customer to a DNC list maintained by Campaign Provider.
  • DNC is per B2B Customer:
    • Customer may opt out for one B2B brand but still receive campaigns for another.
  • Before every campaign launch, Campaign Provider:
    • Applies filters.
    • Removes any DNC customers for that B2B Customer.
Responsibilities Summary
B2B Customer Campaign Provider Genesys Cloud

  • B2B Customer: defines campaign, selects template & filters, launches campaigns, views stats.
  • Campaign Provider: owns data, creates Genesys campaigns, handles callbacks, maintains DNC.
  • Genesys Cloud: manages outbound campaign & calling list, sends WhatsApp messages, triggers callbacks.
