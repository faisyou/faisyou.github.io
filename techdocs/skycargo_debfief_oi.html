Introduction: Air Cargo & Essential Terminologies

  • Air Waybill (AWB): Contractual document for shipment carriage.
  • Volumetric Weight: Cargo space calculation based on dimensions.
  • Chargeable Weight: The greater of volumetric or gross weight, determining pricing.
  • Special Handling Codes (SHC): Indicate cargo requiring unique treatment.
  • MAWB & HAWB: Master and House Air Waybills for consolidated shipments.
  • Unit Load Device (ULD): Containers/pallets designed for air cargo.

Air Cargo vs. Other Transportation Modes

  • Speed: Critical for urgent medical supplies, perishables, and time-sensitive docs.
  • High-Value Cargo: Electronics, pharmaceuticals, jewelry require security and speed.
  • Regulatory Needs: Hazardous materials, fragile goods demand stringent handling.

Air cargo offers fast transit, lower damage risk, and global reach at a premium cost compared to sea, road, or rail.
