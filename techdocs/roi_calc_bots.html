Narrative

Digital is inherently asynchronous (e.g., WhatsApp): agents can juggle multiple sessions concurrently and many “multi‑question” intents are split across separate short sessions. Voice is synchronous: concurrency is ~1 and a caller tends to ask multiple questions inside a single continuous session. When pricing is equal for Digital and Voice, customers pay disproportionately more for Digital outcomes because replacing the same hour of agent labor consumes more sessions—and thus more tokens—on Digital.

  • Virtual Agent (VA): 1 token per 2 sessions → more sessions on Digital ⇒ more tokens per hour than Voice.
  • AI Guides: 1 token per session → the gap widens further.

Implication: Keeping the same price for Voice and Digital is not competitive.
