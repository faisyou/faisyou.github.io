Introduction: Understanding Air Cargo & Essential Terminologies

Air Cargo represents a specialized mode of transporting goods by air, focusing primarily on speed, reliability, and safety. Key terminologies that frontline and back-office teams in cargo operations use include:

Air Waybill (AWB)

Contractual document for shipment carriage.

Volumetric Weight

Cargo space calculation based on dimensions.

Chargeable Weight

The greater of volumetric or gross weight, determining pricing.

Special Handling Codes (SHC)

Indicate cargo requiring unique treatment, like pharmaceuticals.

MAWB & HAWB

Facilitate consolidated shipments by multiple shippers.

Unit Load Device (ULD)

Containers/pallets designed for air cargo.

Air Cargo vs. Other Modes of Transportation

Air cargo is selected over sea, rail, or road transport primarily when:

  • ✓ Speed is critical: Urgent medical supplies, perishable foods, or time-sensitive documents.
  • ✓ High-value cargo: Electronics, jewelry, pharmaceuticals, where security and swift transit justify the premium.
  • ✓ Regulatory or safety requirements: Controlled substances, hazardous materials, or fragile goods demanding stringent handling.

Compared to sea or road freight, air cargo offers reduced transit times, lower risk of damage, higher security, and better global connectivity, albeit typically at higher costs.

Digital Customer Service Opportunities

Using WhatsApp & Web-Messaging to Enhance Customer Experience

Proactively identify and assist customers who may need help using data analytics and AI.

Combine human expertise with AI tools to enhance agent efficiency for complex issues.

For a compliance issue, an AI assists the agent with regulatory data and templates for rapid resolution.
